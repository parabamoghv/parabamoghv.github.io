About Me

Hello.

I am a recent mathematics PhD from The Ohio State University, where my research focused on monoidal categories and categorical groups. During my PhD, I became interested in formal verification and theorem proving, particularly through Lean4. I was first introduced to Lean4 at the SLMath (formerly MSRI) Formalization of Mathematics Summer School. Since then, I have formalized parts of my thesis in Lean4 and currently work as a mathematician at Harmonic.

I teach Engineering Mathematics (MATH 1172) at The Ohio State University and genuinely love interacting with students. Some of my favorite moments in teaching come from the unexpected and creative questions students ask, which often lead to interesting mathematical discussions. I am also interested in data science and machine learning. I enjoy learning the mathematics that underlies these methods and exploring how they can be applied to real-world prediction, analysis, and decision-making. Feel free to explore my selected projects below and reach out if you would like to connect.

Amogh Parab. Coherence and Symmetrization of Categorical Groups. PhD thesis, Department of Mathematics, The Ohio State University, 2025.

Categorical groups are monoidal categories in which every object admits a weak inverse. In this document, we establish and prove a coherence theorem for categorical groups. Furthermore, we show that any categorical group is equivalent, via categorical group functors, to a strict categorical group. Central to our approach is the introduction of a new algebraic structure, the dashed monoid, for which we provide a complete and formal description of the free dashed monoid generated by a set. We conclude by presenting a construction that produces a symmetric categorical group from a given categorical group.

Thesis Formalization in Lean4

I formalized the Dashed Monoid construction from my thesis in Lean4 as a rewrite system for words in a free monoid. I also proved the universal property of the free dashed monoid.
